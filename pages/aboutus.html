There Is Important Nuance To This Race As Well

The Department of Transportation created the first set of guidelines around autonomous vehicles. Their first task: figure out what autonomous even means. Below are their 5 levels with a little explanation. If my little explanations don't seem adequate, you can referernce the actual detailed report from the Department of Transportation for yourself..

  • Level 0: No Automation: Pretty self-explanatory
  • Level 1: Driver Assistance: All of the driving is done by the person, but the car can automatically assist with something (think power-steering).
  • Level 2: Partial Automation Some function of driving can sometimes shift to the driver (think cruise control)
  • Level 3: Conditional Automation: Drivers are still necessary for more extreme sitations but the car is assumed to be functionally autonomous.
  • Level 4: High Automation: This is "fully autonomous" for normal situations. Don't ask this car to merge onto the freeway or drive through the snow for you though... As a side note, my money is on John Deere to get here first.
  • Level 5: Full Automation: For the tech and auto world, the holy grail. This means that your vehicle is just as good as a person in every imaginable scenario
